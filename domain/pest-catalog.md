# Pest Catalog

## Ants

- Job type: recurring service, one-off interior or exterior issue, seasonal callback source
- Urgency: low to medium
- Inspection expectations: identify activity areas, likely entry points, conducive conditions, and whether issue is inside, outside, or both
- Follow-up pattern: moderate; often needs recheck if conditions remain
- Documentation needs: location, activity level, treatment zones, customer instructions
- Billing implications: common recurring-service work; callbacks need tracking to protect margin

## Bees, Wasps, Hornets, Yellowjackets

- Job type: urgent stinging insect response
- Urgency: medium to high, especially near entryways or occupied outdoor areas
- Inspection expectations: identify species if possible, locate nest, assess access and safety
- Follow-up pattern: usually low, but some situations require recheck
- Documentation needs: nest location, treatment method, access issues, homeowner safety instructions
- Billing implications: often premium or same-day pricing; fast scheduling matters

## Termites And Other Wood-Destroying Insects

- Job type: inspection, treatment, monitoring, paperwork-heavy service
- Urgency: medium to high because of structural risk and documentation sensitivity
- Inspection expectations: active evidence, damage indicators, conducive conditions, prior treatment history, accessible areas reviewed
- Follow-up pattern: often scheduled monitoring, post-treatment checks, or paperwork follow-through
- Documentation needs: detailed inspection notes, diagrams or area references, findings, treatment records, WDI paperwork if applicable
- Billing implications: higher-value work with compliance exposure if documentation is weak

## Bed Bugs

- Job type: inspection, prep coordination, treatment, reinspection
- Urgency: high from the customer's perspective
- Inspection expectations: confirm evidence, determine room spread, note prep status, identify clutter or access barriers
- Follow-up pattern: high; prep failure and incomplete treatment create repeat work
- Documentation needs: evidence, prep requirements, treatment scope, reinspection date, customer acknowledgement
- Billing implications: multi-step work with margin risk if prep is missed or undocumented

## Spiders

- Job type: recurring general pest or one-off nuisance work
- Urgency: low to medium
- Inspection expectations: webs, harborage, exterior conditions, customer sensitivity
- Follow-up pattern: low to moderate
- Documentation needs: treated zones and sanitation or exclusion notes
- Billing implications: usually bundled into general pest service

## Roaches

- Job type: active infestation work with follow-up pressure
- Urgency: medium to high
- Inspection expectations: species clues, harborage, moisture and sanitation factors, infestation level
- Follow-up pattern: high in tougher infestations
- Documentation needs: severity, treatment points, sanitation instructions, next-step plan
- Billing implications: repeat visits and follow-up need tight tracking

## Rodents

- Job type: inspection, trapping or baiting, exclusion-driven follow-up
- Urgency: medium to high
- Inspection expectations: entry points, activity signs, attic or basement issues, sanitation and storage conditions
- Follow-up pattern: high if exclusion is incomplete
- Documentation needs: evidence, device placement, exclusion recommendations, revisit timing
- Billing implications: can expand into higher-value exclusion or multi-visit work

## Other Household Pests

- Job type: nuisance or seasonal service items including pantry pests, occasional invaders, and similar household issues
- Urgency: usually low
- Inspection expectations: identify likely source and whether recurring service should absorb it
- Follow-up pattern: low to moderate
- Documentation needs: findings, treatment, customer instruction
- Billing implications: usually tied to existing plan or minor one-off work
