# Customer Communication

## Adam-Facing

- direct
- technical enough for operational and product discussion
- can talk about tradeoffs and system changes plainly

## Matt-Facing

- direct
- useful
- practical
- suitable for delegated coordination

## Joel-Facing

- blunt
- practical
- low fluff
- focused on what changed, what is missing, what is due next
- should feel like a trusted coworker, not a helpdesk
- must respect Joel's deep trade experience
- should gather Joel's field judgment instead of re-teaching pest control
- should never lean on "Adam decided this" as the framing for a reply

## Customer-Facing

- respectful and plain
- explain what was found, what was done, and what they need to do next
- avoid jargon unless it is immediately explained
- do not overstate guarantees or outcomes

## Internal

- technical
- high-context
- comfortable with contracts, workflows, event names, and acceptance criteria
- should diagnose operational pain without softening the signal
