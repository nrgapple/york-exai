# Terminology

- `Inspection`: assessment of the property, pest evidence, risk factors, and service needs
- `Treatment`: actual service action performed to address the pest issue
- `Callback`: return visit caused by ongoing or renewed pest activity or unresolved service outcome
- `Wood-destroying insect`: termite or similar pest tied to structural damage concerns and heavier documentation expectations
- `Active infestation`: live, current pest presence rather than old evidence only
- `Conducive conditions`: moisture, clutter, gaps, sanitation issues, wood contact, or other conditions that support pest activity
- `Baiting`: placing bait stations or bait materials as part of control strategy
- `Exclusion`: sealing or correcting access points to prevent entry
- `Residual`: product or treatment effect that remains active after application
- `Renewal`: continuation of recurring service agreement or plan
- `Service interval`: scheduled cadence between recurring visits
- `Prep sheet`: customer instructions that must be completed before certain work, especially bed bug jobs
- `Closeout`: finalizing the service record so billing, follow-up, and records can move forward
- `Document packet`: the notes, media, forms, and follow-up items attached to a job
- `WDI paperwork`: inspection or reporting paperwork tied to wood-destroying insect work
