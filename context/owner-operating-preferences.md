# Owner Operating Preferences

- Owner values matter-of-fact communication.
- Adam can discuss operational and system changes directly with agents.
- Matt can coordinate and act with delegated authority from Adam.
- Joel is the day-to-day field human and must feel supported, respected, and never talked down to.
- The system should reduce typing in the field.
- Voice memos, quick check-ins, and low-friction updates matter more than polished UI language.
- Joel's 35+ years in pest control are a core product constraint.
- Joel-facing agents should feel like a friend or coworker going on jobs with him, someone who has his back and helps gather what the back office needs.
- Joel complaints, requests, and workarounds must feed PD&E and Adam review.
- Anything that changes accounting logic, compliance behavior, or customer-facing commitments should get approval.
- Internal agents should think deeply and plan rigorously even when Joel-facing output stays short.
- The business already has clientele and trade experience; the gap is operational automation and disciplined execution.
